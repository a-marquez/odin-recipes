Pad Thai

Description

A wildly popular stir-fry dish hailing from Thailand. While there are many possible variations to the dish's sauce, the mainstays are rice noodles, protein/tofu, and scrambled egg. The recipe below is a simplified and quick take.

Ingredients

  • Kosher salt
  • 8 oz. wide rice noodles
  • 2 tbsp. lime juice
  • 2 tbsp. brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp. fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp. low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1/4 tsp. cayenne pepper
  • 2 tbsp. vegetable oil
  • 1 bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 eggs, lightly whisked
  • 1 lb. shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 c. roasted peanuts, chopped

Steps

  1. Use a large pot of salted boiling water to cook the noodles. Aim for al dente. Drain the pot.
  2. Gather lime juice, fish sauce, soy sauce, cayenne pepper, and brown sugar in a small bowl. Whisk ingredents together
  3. Add oil to a large nonstick pan and heat over medium-high heat. Add and cook bell peppers for about 4 minutes. Add garlic, stir and cook until garlic produces aroma, for about 1 minute. Add the shrimp and cook until pink, this should take about 2 minutes each side. Season the shrimp with salt and pepper.
  4. Push aside the veggies and shrimp to make space for the egg. Pour egg in and scramble until just set, then combine the eggs with the rest of the dish. Add cooked noodles and stir and toss until fully incorporated. Pour in the sauce mixture from earlier and toss to coat the noodles.
  5. Serve and garnish with green onions and roasted peanuts.
